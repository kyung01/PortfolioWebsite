Reflective Shadow Maps

demo based on paper by Casten Daschsbacher and Marc Stamminger

It is pseudo global illumination method. Easy(compared to other global illumination methods) and cheap to impelemnt.


This image dispalys the best scenario. Few objects with smooth surfaces. The reflection pattern is rather irragular in the scene. This is due to the fact that I do not use well pre-calculated sampling coordinates. Paper described to use circular sampling pattern that is dense around rendering pixel. However I use sampling pattern in circular motion without being dense around rendering pixel.

The reflection is first rendered at low resolution. Then higher quality reflection is rendered if needed. The red pixels represent spots where higher quality reflection rendering process is required. More these red pixels are, more intense the rendering process gets.

Here is an objcet with very rough normals. These detailed look requie higher quality reflection. As the result the low resolution reflection cannot be used to most surfaces.

As you can see, most of the object is colored red. Red being insufficient detail at low resolition version.

However as you get closer to the object, the details get less intense as they become less dense. Thus the low resolution reflection can be used for these pixels.

This method however cannot see "obstacles" blocking the ray's path. In the example, the green light bouncing off of green wall is being casted onto all three red blocks though the ray is supposed to be blocked after it hits closest red block.
